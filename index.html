    Valamana, officially the Democratic Union of Valamana, is a country located in South Asia. It consists of land formerly belonging to India, Bangladesh, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka. It has a coastline along the Indian Ocean and the Bay of Bengal. It borders Iran and Afghanistan to the west, Nepal, Bhutan, Tajikstan, and China to the north, and Myanmar to the east. It is the third-largest country behind China and the United States, making it the second-largest republic and the largest parliamentary republic.

    Valamana was formed on 3 September 2032 in the aftermath of World War III. The country has been in a developing period for all f its history, with the country trying to emerge as a country comparable to those in the West. The country formed its government after European governments, keeping the same system in place in Sri Lanka. Since its forming, concerns of overpopulation have risen, and the government has tried to solve these problems, trying various programs with varying levels of success. Today, these concerns have been lowered as the population has halved since the country's creation.

    History

    In the aftermath of World War III, Sri Lanka and Pakistan were on the verge of collapse, with Bangladesh and India remaining stable but fearing a possible collapse. Politicians from Sri Lanka and Pakistan were concerned and explored a merger of the two countries. Realizing a union would cause more harm than good, both countries tried to get Bangladesh, India, and Afghanistan to join, promising prosperity and opportunities. Valamana was formed at the Mumbai Summit in 2032, where leaders advocated for a merger of South Asian countries at the recommendation of the people in various referendums. Afghanistan was initially planned to be included in the pact, and Afghan voters had lent their support to the cause, but Afghan leaders opposed a merger and the plan fell apart. Prakash Upadhyay was elected the first President of Valamana and Shanti Randhawa was elected Prime Minister.

    After the merger, Valamana became the most populous country in the world, and politicians from Bangladesh, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka raised concerns about overpopulation and Indian political dominance. These politicians threatened defiance if these concerns were not levied. In 2040, to dampen the number of Hindi Indians in Valamana, the government implemented a program giving Indians more opportunities to leave the country and go to Europe and the United States. This program enjoyed moderate success, but it took a policy prohibiting Indians from parenting a child, implemented in 2045. Many human rights organizations were concerned about this policy and the Council of Nations considered sanctions against Valamana, but Director of Human Affairs Amit Chattopadhyay bargained for nations to welcome in more Indian families. This offer was accepted and the population of Valamana slowly declined over the next fifty years. Valamana has enjoyed political diversity every since, with the first non-Indian president being elected in 2070.

    Valamana has been active in diplomatic efforts, joining the Council of Nations just months after its formation. The country was also invited to the G10 summit in 2035. It currently sits on the Security Department of the Council of Nations.

    In 2082, the Valamani Civil War Scare faced the nation, with the working class demanding better wages from their employers. The employers mostly lived in the rich province of Narsinghat. The government of Narsinghat declared a state of provincial emergency when uprisings arose in the province. The Scare ended when the Valamani government passed measures giving workers more freedom, the measure was passed mere hours before the working class was supposed to start fighting.

    Valamana is currently undergoing a political crisis. Following the 2125 Elections, Palak Mahanta was declared the official winner of the election. However, videos were leaked suggesting the election workers changed the results of the election. Ali Akbar Mughal was expected by many to win the election, and he alleges that Mahanta paid off election workers in exchange for their support in the upcoming election. A commission was set up to investigate the election, said investigation is currently ongoing.

    Politics

    The government of Valamana works similar to that of the United States and Canada. The Parliament of Valamana is not divided into chambers, and each voting district has 1,000,000 constituents. The Parliament is the legislative branch of Valamana, and all measured passed by the Parliament gets sent to the President for him to sign off on. Should the President veto a measure, that measure is added to the next yearly ballot for the voters to vote on. If the measure is marked as urgent by the Prime Minister, the measure is sent back to the Parliament where they can override the veto with a 75% majority. The Office of the Presidency is currently suspended due to the 2125 Political Crisis. Rushil Neerthihan was named by both Mahanta and Mughal as the Commissioner to investigate the 2125 Election. Neerthihan currently serves as the acting president while the office is suspended.

    Voting is held every June in Valamana. Every five years, a presidential election is held, and every two years, legislative elections are held. On odd-numbered years not divisible by five, voter measures are on the ballot. There are two main parties: Valamana People and Democratic Union. The Valamana People Party consists of mostly Hindi persons, while the Democratic Union mainly consists of everyone else, with some exceptions.

    Provinces

    Valamana is divided into five provinces: Narsinghat, Nagardu, Selagaginna, Kahtabad, and Manthan. The provinces do not have legislatures, instead, each province sends its premier to Parliament as a non-voting member. The premier also has the power to invoke certain powers, such as declaring a state of emergency, in order to get more funding from the government.

    Geography

    Valamana spans most of the Indian subcontinent, with Silabani taking up the rest of the subcontinent. The country borders the Indian Ocean and the Bay of Bengal, with the island of Sri Lanka sitting in said bay. The mountain ranges of Hindu Kush, Spīn Ghar (Safed Koh), Sulaiman Mountains, and Kirthar Mountains reach into Valamana, creating a border of sorts into the country. Due to these mountain ranges, people from the north and west are unable to get into Valamana via foot.

    Climate

    Valamana, sitting near the equator, has a very tropical climate. Temperatures range from 20 °C to 40 °C. Valamana experiences brutal monsoons every year, requiring innovative strategies to protect its citizens. Citizens are required to stay inside during monsoon months, with government officials delivering necessary materials to survive. During warmer months, the government mandates that all air conditioning units be checked.

    Demographics

    Valamana has approximately 500,000,000 residents. This population declines at a rate of 1,000 per year, but the rate of this decline is slowly decreasing, and scientists expect the population to start growing in about ten years. Population is more dense in the north, as most upper class citizens reside in the north. Most citizens are Hindi, with 41% belonging to this group. 23% are Bengali, 13% are Sinhalese, 10% are Tamil and 10% are Punjabi. Very few people of other ethnicities reside in Valamana, with about 1.5% of the population being East Asian, 1.4% being African, and 0.1% being White. Religions praticed in Valamana include Buddhism, Hinduism, Islam, and Christianity. Disputes within Parliament have prevented Valamana from obtaining one official language. Hindi, Sinhalese, Urdu, Bengali, and Tamil are the official languages of Valamana, but most citizens know some English, which is used in public spaces to communicate. Advocacy groups for all of these languages have tried to get their language to become the official language of Valamana, but politicians are hesistant to push for an official language, fearing alienation of their voterbase.

    Culture

    Main articles: Sri Lankan cuisine, Indian cuisine, Pakistani cuisine, and Bangladeshi cuisine

    Valamani culture is heavily influenced by region, which each ethnicity in Valamana has its own culture. Valamana has culture festivals every year in each province where citizens can try new foods and traditions. Most religious holidays are nationally observed and are times of prayer and reflection. Most Valamani citizens have played cricket, including politicians, but other sports played include rugby and watersports. The Valamani National Cricket Team has won five World Cups (2043, 2067, 2079, 2103, 2107).
