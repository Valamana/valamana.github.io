    Valamana, officially the Democratic Union of Valamana, is a country located in South Asia. It has a coastline along the Indian Ocean and the Bay of Bengal. It borders Pakistan to the west and India to the east.

    Valamanian land was ceded by Pakistan on 18 June 1962 and by India on 3 September 1962 to officially form the country.

    History

    The land which now belongs to Valamana was purchased and settled by elites in 1956, for a price of ₹100 billion ($700 billion) with the goal of creating a land comparable to that in the middle east or in the west. The land owners enticed people to move to the land with cash offers as well as discounted housing and other goods. Politicians were encouraged to be a part of the Valamanian parliament which would act as a provincial government. Prakash Upadhyay was appointed the first President of Valamana and Shanti Randhawa was appointed Prime Minister. The land owners made a proportion of the amount that the government made until 1994, when they made back four times their investment.

    In 1962, the Valamanian government and the Pakistani and Indian governments disagreed over Valamanian practices and the proportion of taxes that Valamana owed each government. Valamanian officials threatened revolution and withheld all payments to the governments. An agreement was reached with Pakistan where they would pay Pakistan ₹25 billion ($175 billion) every year for three years in exchange for the cession of land. India refused this deal, and offered cession of some Valamani land, which was accepted. Months later, India ceded the rest of Valamani land. 

    In 1982, the Valamani Civil War Scare faced the nation, with the working class demanding better wages from their employers. The employers mostly lived in the rich province of Narsinghat. The government of Narsinghat declared a state of provincial emergency when uprisings arose in the province. The Scare ended when the Valamani government passed measures giving workers more freedom, the measure was passed mere hours before the working class was supposed to start fighting.

    Valamana is currently undergoing a political crisis. Following the 2044 Elections, Palak Mahanta was declared the official winner of the election. However, videos were leaked suggesting the election workers changed the results of the election. Ali Akbar Mughal was expected by many to win the election, and he alleges that Mahanta paid off election workers in exchange for their support in the upcoming election. A commission was set up to investigate the election, said investigation is currently ongoing.

    Politics

    Valamana is a parliamentary republic. The Parliament of Valamana is not divided into chambers, and each voting district has 1,000,000 constituents. The Parliament is the legislative branch of Valamana, and all measured passed by the Parliament gets sent to the President for them to sign off on. Should the President veto a measure, that measure is added to the next yearly ballot for the voters to vote on. If the measure is marked as urgent by the Prime Minister, the measure is sent back to the Parliament where they can override the veto with a 75% majority. The Office of the Presidency is currently suspended due to the 2044 Political Crisis. Rushil Neerthihan was named by both Mahanta and Mughal as the Commissioner to investigate the 2044 Election. Neerthihan currently serves as the acting president while the office is suspended.

    Voting is held every July in Valamana. Every four years, a presidential election is held, and every two years, legislative elections are held. There are two main parties: Valamana People and Democratic Union. The Valamana People Party consists of mostly Hindi persons, while the Democratic Union mainly consists of everyone else, with some exceptions.

    2044 Election

    On 1 July 2044, the presidential election was held in Valamana. President Anil Mazumdar of the Democratic Union (DU) was term-limited, and his prime minister, Ali Akbar Mughal, was widely seen as the favorite to become the next president. Mughal entered the election on 3 February 2043. Palak Mahanta of the Valamana People Party (VPP) entered the election on 17 December 2042. Mahanta was originally seen as a long shot but gained widespread support from moderates who were upset with the DU's progressive social policies. She ran on what some people called the most socially conservative VPP platform ever. Polling numbers were listed as 62% for Mughal and 34% for Mahanta by the Asian Polling Group. Mahanta was declared the winner the morning after polls closed, with the gap between the two candidates at 406,282, the smallest gap between two candidates in any Valamanian election. Mughal conceded the election immediately after media outlets reported Mahanta won. Mazumdar also congratulated Mahanta and began preparations for her to take over on 1 October.

    On 14 August, footage was released on Bluesky purportedly depicting election workers deliberately changing election results. A whistleblower alleged that Mahanta bribed election officials to change election results. The footage was not verified by any party implicated. Mahanta denied the allegations and the election workers declined to comment on the matter, saying "we will wait until an investigation has concluded to comment". Mughal retracted his concession and sued Mahanta and the election workers for fraud. The Valamanian Supreme Court ruled that parliament had the authority to nullify an election result during the three-month lame-duck period. On 16 September, Mughal and Mahanta agreed to appoint centrist Peaceful Valamana Party (PVP) former MP Rushil Neerthihan to investigate the election. On 23 September, Parliament nullified the results of the election pending investigation and established the office of the Commissioner of Election Investigation, which carries the powers of the presidency and the premiership.

    Demographics

    Valamana has approximately 100,000,000 residents. Population is more dense in the west, as most upper class citizens reside in the west, due to the earlier cession of West Valamana. Most citizens are Hindi, with 41% belonging to this group. 23% are Bengali, 13% are Sinhalese, 10% are Tamil and 10% are Punjabi. Very few people of other ethnicities reside in Valamana, with about 1.5% of the population being East Asian, 1.4% being African, and 0.1% being White. Religions praticed in Valamana include Buddhism, Hinduism, Islam, and Christianity. Disputes within Parliament have prevented Valamana from obtaining one official language. Hindi, Sinhalese, Urdu, Bengali, and Tamil are the official languages of Valamana, but most citizens know some English, which is used in public spaces to communicate. Advocacy groups for all of these languages have tried to get their language to become the official language of Valamana, but politicians are hesistant to push for an official language, fearing alienation of their voterbase. Hindi is the most used language and is required to be taught at schools, but at least one other language must be taught in well.

    Culture

    Main articles: Sri Lankan cuisine, Indian cuisine, Pakistani cuisine, and Bangladeshi cuisine

    Valamani culture is heavily influenced by region, with each ethnicity in Valamana having its own culture. Valamana has cultural festivals every year in each province where citizens can try new foods and traditions. Most religious holidays are nationally observed and are times of prayer and reflection. Most Valamani citizens have played cricket, including politicians, but other sports played include rugby and watersports. The Valamanian National Cricket Team has won three World Cups (1983, 2007, 2035).
